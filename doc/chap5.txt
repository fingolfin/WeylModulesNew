  
  [1X5 [33X[0;0YCase studies[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YIn  this  chapter we give a few examples of the package in use, by obtaining
  information  about certain Weyl modules. This is intended to illustrate some
  of the possibilities and demonstrate how the commands can work together.[133X
  
  
  [1X5.1 [33X[0;0YType G 2 in characteristic 2[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YIn  this  section  we  study  a  few  small  Weyl  modules  for  type [23XG_2[123X in
  characteristic  [23X2[123X. We will show that the Weyl module of highest weight [23X[2,0][123X
  has four composition factors and is non-rigid (meaning that its socle series
  does not coincide with its radical series).[133X
  
  
  [1X5.1-1 [33X[0;0YDimensions of restricted simples[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThere  are  four  restricted weights in this situation: [23X[0,0][123X, [23X[1,0][123X, [23X[0,1][123X,
  and  [23X[1,1][123X. Let's start by computing the dimensions of the Weyl modules with
  those highest weights.[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XV00 := WeylModule(2,[0,0],"G",2); Dim(V00);[127X[104X

    [4X[28X1[128X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XV10:=WeylModule(2,[1,0],"G",2); Dim(V10);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X<Type G2 Weyl module of highest weight [ 1, 0 ] at prime p = 2>[128X[104X
    [4X[28X7[128X[104X

  [33X[0;0YOf  course  we  know that [10XV00[110X is the trivial module and [10XV11[110X is the Steinberg
  module;  they  are both simple. It turns out that [10XV01[110X is also simple but [10XV10[110X
  is not.[133X

  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0YThis  suggests  that  [10XV10[110X is a non-split extension of its simple head by the
  trivial  module.  If  so,  the  simple  module  of  highest weight [23X[1,0][123X has
  dimension [23X6[123X. We can double check that guess, as follows.[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X

  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0YThis  confirms  our  guess.  The  simple  head of [10XV10[110X must have dimension [23X6[123X.
  Another  way  to  confirm  it  is to compute the simple character of highest
  weight [23X[1,0][123X.[133X

  [1X5.1-2 [33X[0;0YStructure of the Weyl module of highest weight $[2,0]$[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YNow  we  turn  to an analysis of the structure of the Weyl module of highest
  weight [23X[2,0][123X.[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XV20 := WeylModule(2,[2,0],"G",2); Dim(V20); [127X[104X

  the generator of [10XV20[110X.[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X

    [4X[28X<SubWeylModule of dimension 6, generated by elements [ y4*v0 ] of weights [128X[104X
    [4X[28X[ [ 1, 0 ] ]>, in[128X[104X
    [4X[28X<Type G2 Weyl module of highest weight [ 2, 0 ] at prime p = 2>[128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0YBoth submodules are simple, hence lie in the socle. In fact, we have[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X

    [4X[28X<SubWeylModule of dimension 20, generated by elements [128X[104X

  [33X[0;0Ywhich confirms this.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  corresponding  quotient by the socle is thus a [23X7[123X-dimensional module. It
  would be useful to compute the socle of this quotient.[133X

  [1X5.2 [33X[0;0YType A 2[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YWe analyze the structure of some Weyl modules in type [23XA_2[123X.[133X
  
  
  [1X5.2-1 [33X[0;0YThe Weyl module of highest weight $[7,7]$ in characteristic 2[133X[101X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X

  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0YIn  characteristic [23X2[123X this is a simple Weyl module. In fact it is a Steinberg
  module.[133X
  
  
  [1X5.2-2 [33X[0;0YThe Weyl module of highest weight $[7,7]$ in characteristic 3[133X[101X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XV77 := WeylModule(3,[7,7],"A",2);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X<Type A2 Weyl module of highest weight [ 7, 7 ] at prime p = 3>[128X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XDecompositionNumbers(V77);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X[ [ 7, 7 ], 1, [ 6, 6 ], 1, [ 3, 9 ], 1, [ 9, 3 ], 1, [ 4, 7 ], 1, [ 7, 4 ], [128X[104X
    [4X[28X  1, [ 3, 6 ], 2, [ 6, 3 ], 2, [ 3, 3 ], 1, [ 0, 0 ], 1 ][128X[104X

    [4X[28X20[128X[104X

  [1X5.2-3 [33X[0;0YThe Weyl module of highest weight $[7,7]$ in characteristic 5[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThis Weyl module illustrates one of Jantzen's generic decomposition patterns
  when the characteristic [23Xp[123X is sufficiently large and the highest weight is in
  the lowest [23Xp^2[123X alcove and sufficiently far away from its walls.[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
