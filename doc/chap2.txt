  
  [1X2 [33X[0;0YWeyl modules[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThis  chapter discusses the commands available for computations (in positive
  characteristic  [23Xp[123X)  with  Weyl modules, quotient Weyl modules, submodules of
  Weyl  modules,  and submodules of quotient Weyl modules. Here the underlying
  Lie algebra is simply-connected and simple.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YWARNING. In most cases, the dimension of space of maximal vectors of a given
  dominant  weight  is  either [23X0[123X or [23X1[123X. Cases for which there exist two or more
  independent  maximal  vectors  of the same weight can lead to complications,
  such  as  a lack of rigidity in the submodule structure. Such situations are
  relatively rare (and interesting). An example in Type [23XD_4[123X is the Weyl module
  of  highest  weight  [23X[0,1,0,0][123X, as pointed out on page 173 of [CPS75]. (I am
  grateful to Anton Cox for this reference.)[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YA  Weyl module (as in the previous paragraph) with at least one weight space
  containing   multiple   linearly   independent  maximal  vectors  is  called
  [13Xambiguous[113X. Extra care is needed when studying ambiguous modules.[133X
  
  
  [1X2.1 [33X[0;0YConstructors[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YHere  we  consider  functions  that  create  Weyl modules, quotients of Weyl
  modules, submodules of Weyl modules, and submodules of quotient Weyl modules
  (subquotients).[133X
  
  
  [1X2.1-1 [33X[0;0YWeylModule[133X[101X
  
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XWeylModule[102X( [3Xp[103X, [3Xwt[103X, [3Xt[103X, [3Xr[103X ) [32X operation[133X
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XWeylModule[102X( [3XV[103X, [3Xwt[103X ) [32X operation[133X
  [6XReturns:[106X  [33X[0;10Ya WeylModule[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe first form of the command (with four arguments) constructs a Weyl module
  of  highest  weight  [3Xwt[103X in characteristic [3Xp[103X of type [3Xt[103X and rank [3Xr[103X. The second
  form of the command (with two arguments) constructs a Weyl module of highest
  weight  [3Xwt[103X of the same characteristic and root system as the first parameter
  [3XV[103X, which must be an existing Weyl module.[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XV:= WeylModule(2,[1,0],"A",2);[127X[104X
    [4X[28XV[ 1, 0 ][128X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XW:= WeylModule(V,[1,2]);[127X[104X
    [4X[28XV[ 1, 2 ][128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0YWeyl  modules  are attribute-storing objects (they remember their attributes
  after the first time they are computed).[133X
  
  
  [1X2.1-2 [33X[0;0YQuotientWeylModule[133X[101X
  
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XQuotientWeylModule[102X( [3XS[103X ) [32X operation[133X
  [6XReturns:[106X  [33X[0;10Ya QuotientWeylModule[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YConstructs  the  quotient module [23XV/S[123X corresponding to the given submodule [3XS[103X.
  Here [9XV[109X is the ambient Weyl module of the given submodule [3XS[103X.[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XW:= WeylModule(2,[2,0],"A",2);[127X[104X
    [4X[28XV[ 2, 0 ][128X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XQ:= QuotientWeylModule(SocleWeyl(W));[127X[104X
    [4X[28X3-dimensional quotient of V[ 2, 0 ][128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0YQuotient Weyl modules are attribute-storing objects.[133X
  
  
  [1X2.1-3 [33X[0;0YSubWeylModule[133X[101X
  
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XSubWeylModule[102X( [3XV[103X, [3Xv[103X ) [32X operation[133X
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XSubWeylModule[102X( [3XS[103X, [3Xv[103X ) [32X operation[133X
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XSubWeylModule[102X( [3XV[103X, [3Xlist[103X ) [32X operation[133X
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XSubWeylModule[102X( [3XS[103X, [3Xlist[103X ) [32X operation[133X
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XSubWeylModuleDirectSum[102X( [3XV[103X, [3Xlist[103X ) [32X operation[133X
  [6XReturns:[106X  [33X[0;10Ya SubWeylModule[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YSubmodules  are  attribute-storing  objects.  The  first form constructs the
  submodule  of  the given Weyl module [3XV[103X generated by the given element [3Xv[103X. The
  second  form constructs the submodule (of the ambient Weyl module) generated
  by  the given submodule [3XS[103X and given element [3Xv[103X. The third form constructs the
  submodule  of the given Weyl module [3XV[103X generated by the given element [3Xlist[103X of
  elements.  The  fourth  form constructs the submodule generated by the given
  submodule  [3XS[103X  and  given  [3Xlist[103X  of  elements.  The fifth form constructs the
  submodule  of the Weyl module [3XV[103X which is the direct sum of the given list of
  submodules (which are assumed to be linearly independent, without checking).[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XW:= WeylModule(2,[2,0],"A",2);[127X[104X
    [4X[28XV[ 2, 0 ][128X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27Xm:= MaximalVectors(W);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X[ 1*v0, y1*v0 ][128X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XS:= SubWeylModule(W, m[2]);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X3-dimensional submod of V[ 2, 0 ][128X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XSubWeylModule(S, m[1]);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X6-dimensional submod of V[ 2, 0 ][128X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XSubWeylModule(W, m);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X6-dimensional submod of V[ 2, 0 ][128X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XSubWeylModule(S, m);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X6-dimensional submod of V[ 2, 0 ][128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  
  [1X2.1-4 [33X[0;0YSubQuotientWeylModule[133X[101X

  the  subquotient  Weyl module generated by the given subquotient [3XS[103X and given
  [3Xlist[103X  of  elements.  The fifth form constructs the submodule of the quotient
  Weyl  module  [3XQ[103X  which  is  the direct sum of the given [3Xlist[103X of subquotients

    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XS:= SubWeylModule(Q,m[1]);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X3-dimensional submod of 3-dimensional quotient of V[ 2, 0 ][128X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XSubWeylModule(Q,m);[127X[104X
